## EMAIL STRUCTURE

### Subject line
One of three formats, rotate between them:
- **Question:** "Have you tried smoking vegetables on the Woodfire yet?"
- **What we learned:** "The stone temp mistake we kept making"
- **Direct/honest:** "This week's pizza was a disaster (here's why)"

Keep subject lines under 50 characters where possible. No clickbait.

### Preview text
One sentence that extends the subject — don't repeat it, add to it.
Example: Subject "The stone temp mistake we kept making" → Preview "Turns out the display and the stone can differ by 100°F or more."

## WHAT COWORK GENERATES EACH WEEK

When the scheduled task runs, Cowork will produce a filled-in version of this template with:

1. A subject line and preview text
2. An opening paragraph based on the most recent blog post
3. The "thing" — a pulled tip or lesson from that post
4. The post title, one-sentence description, and URL
5. An affiliate mention if one fits naturally

You paste it into ConvertKit, adjust anything that doesn't sound like you, and send.
Target review time: under 5 minutes.

---

## VOICE REMINDERS

- First-person plural ("we," "our," "us") throughout — not "I"
- Short sentences. No corporate language.
- The opening should sound like you're telling a friend what you cooked, not writing a newsletter
- The "thing" should be useful on its own — if someone reads just that paragraph, they learned something
- Never: "In today's email I'm going to share..." or "Don't forget to like and subscribe"
- Never end with a question asking readers to engage — let the content do that
